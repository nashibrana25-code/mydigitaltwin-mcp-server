## Job Research Workflow

### Step 1: Seek.com.au Search Process

1. Go to https://www.seek.com.au/
2. Use search terms above
3. **Filters to Apply:**
   - Location: Sydney NSW (or Remote)
   - Work type: Full-time, Part-time, Casual (depending on category)
   - Salary: $50K-$80K (or "Any")
   - Posted: Last 7 days (fresher postings)

4. **Selection Criteria:**
   - ✅ Entry-level or Junior (0-2 years experience)
   - ✅ SQL + Python mentioned
   - ✅ Training/mentorship offered
   - ✅ Student visa friendly (don't explicitly require citizenship)
   - ❌ Avoid: 3+ years experience, senior roles, security clearance required

---

### Step 2: Save Each Job

For each job you find:

```powershell
# Create new job file
New-Item "job-research/job-postings/job-[NUMBER]-[short-title].md"

# Copy template
Get-Content "job-research/job-collection-template.md" | Set-Content "job-research/job-postings/job-[NUMBER]-[short-title].md"
```

Then fill in:
- Copy entire job description from Seek
- Complete Personal Match Analysis section
- Add notes about company research
- Rate match score (1-10)

### Tips for Efficient Collection:

**Time Management:**
- Allocate 15-20 minutes per job (search + save + analyze)
- Do 2-3 jobs per session
- Complete 10+ jobs over 3-4 sessions

**Quality Over Quantity:**
- Better to deeply analyze 10 good-fit jobs than collect 50 poorly matched roles
- Focus on roles you'd genuinely accept if offered
- Prioritize companies with good culture/training programs

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- ❌ "Must have security clearance" (you can't get as international student)
- ❌ "5+ years experience required"
- ❌ "Expert in [specific enterprise tool]" with no training mentioned
- ❌ Roles requiring Australian citizenship

## Research Tips

**Company Research:**
- Check Glassdoor reviews for company culture
- Look at LinkedIn company page for employee experiences
- Google "[Company] graduate program reviews"
- Check if they sponsor visas for future (when you graduate)

**Application Timeline:**
- Most graduate programs: Applications open Feb-Apr, start dates Feb next year
- Internships: Rolling basis, apply as soon as you see them
- Part-time/Casual: Apply year-round

**Student Visa Considerations:**
- Be upfront about work hour limitations (48hrs/fortnight)
- Mention unlimited hours during uni breaks (great for internships)
- Emphasize commitment and reliability despite visa status
- Many companies value international perspective
