## üéØ PART 3: Industry-Specific Technical Knowledge Bank

### Finance Concepts You Must Know

#### **1. Variance Analysis**
**Definition:** Comparing actual financial results against forecasted/budgeted amounts to identify discrepancies

**Types:**
- **Price Variance:** Difference due to price changes
- **Volume Variance:** Difference due to quantity changes
- **Mix Variance:** Difference due to product mix changes

**How to discuss:**
"Variance analysis helps identify where operations deviated from plan. For inventory variance at Samsung, I'd compare actual inventory costs vs. budgeted costs, break down variances by product line, investigate root causes with supply chain teams, and recommend corrective actions."

---

#### **2. Forecasting Methods**
- **Time Series Analysis:** Using historical data trends
- **Regression Analysis:** Identifying relationships between variables
- **Rolling Forecasts:** Continuous forecasting (e.g., 3-month rolling)
- **Scenario Planning:** Best case / base case / worst case

**Your technical edge:** "I can implement forecasting models using Python (Pandas, statsmodels) or Excel, making forecasts more data-driven and automated than manual methods."

---

### Supply Chain & Operations Basics

Since you'll work with supply chain managers:

**Key Terms:**
- **Aging Inventory:** Inventory sitting unsold for extended periods (risk of obsolescence)
- **Inventory Turnover:** How quickly inventory is sold and replaced
